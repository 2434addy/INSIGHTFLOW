# InsightFlow — Market Research

## 1. Market Overview

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- **Global digital marketing agency market:** ~$500B (2025)
- **Marketing analytics & reporting tools segment:** ~$6.5B (2025), growing at 14% CAGR
- **AI in marketing market:** ~$35B (2025), growing at 28% CAGR

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- ~150,000 marketing agencies in English-speaking markets (US, UK, CA, AU)
- Average agency manages 15–40 clients
- Reporting is a universal pain point across all agency sizes

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1
- Target: Small-to-mid agencies (5–50 clients)
- Estimated ~80,000 agencies in this segment
- Goal: 500 paying agencies in Year 1 = 0.6% penetration

## 2. Industry Pain Points

### Time Drain
- Agencies spend 5–10 hours per week per account manager on reporting
- Average agency with 5 account managers = 25–50 hours/week on reports
- At $75/hr blended rate = $97K–195K/year in lost productivity per agency

### Data Fragmentation
- Average client uses 3–5 marketing platforms
- No single tool unifies data across Meta, Google, Shopify, and GA4 seamlessly
- Manual cross-platform analysis is error-prone

### Insight Gap
- Raw data exports lack narrative and context
- Clients want "what does this mean?" not just numbers
- Most tools show dashboards but don't generate written reports

### Client Communication
- Clients expect polished, branded reports
- Report formatting is tedious and repetitive
- Inconsistent quality across team members

## 3. Target Customer Profile

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
- **Company type:** Digital marketing agency or performance marketing firm
- **Size:** 5–100 employees, managing 10–200 clients
- **Services:** Paid media (Meta Ads, Google Ads), e-commerce, SEO, analytics
- **Current tools:** Google Sheets, Google Slides, manual exports, possibly a dashboarding tool
- **Budget:** $200–$1,000/month for reporting tools
- **Decision maker:** Agency Owner, Director of Operations, Head of Client Services

### Early Adopter Signals
- Currently using Google Slides or manual processes for reporting
- Active in agency communities (Agency Hackers, Digital Marketer, agency Slack groups)
- Managing 10+ clients across multiple platforms
- Team of 3+ account managers

## 4. Market Trends

### AI Adoption in Marketing
- 72% of marketers plan to increase AI tool usage in 2025–2026
- Report generation is among the top 3 AI use cases for agencies
- Agencies are willing to pay premium for tools that save analyst time

### Consolidation of Marketing Stacks
- Agencies moving from 10+ point solutions to fewer integrated platforms
- "All-in-one" tools gaining market share over specialists
- API-first tools preferred for integration flexibility

### Client Expectations Rising
- Clients expect real-time or near-real-time data access
- Interactive reports preferred over static PDFs
- Demand for actionable insights, not just data dumps

## 7. Key Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| API rate limits from ad platforms | High | Medium | Implement smart caching, request batching |
| AI hallucination in reports | Medium | High | Data grounding, human review workflow, accuracy checks |
| Low switching cost (easy to churn) | Medium | High | Deep integrations, historical data lock-in, team workflows |
| Competitor copies AI features | High | Medium | Move fast, build moat through UX and integrations |
| Ad platform API changes | Medium | Medium | Abstraction layer, rapid response team |
